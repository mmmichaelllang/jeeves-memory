Theological Physics and Triadic Ontology

I have been attending to your ongoing interest in triadic ontology and its intersections with Trinitarian philosophy, Sir, and I find the scholarly field in a state of genuine productive ferment.

Verlag Karl Alber has launched a dedicated series titled "Studies on Triadic Ontology and Trinitarian Philosophy," edited by a consortium including Enders, Falque, Milbank, Coda, and Rojek. The series explicitly positions itself as moving beyond traditional metaphysics toward what it calls a genuinely triadic understanding of being — encompassing what it terms Triadic Ontology, Triadic Phenomenology, Analogia Trinitatis, Trinitarian Logic, and Sophiology. The editorial ambition is not modest: the series invites work arguing that the triadic structure uniquely sustains unity without collapse, distinction without division, and self-giving without external dependence — framing the ground of being itself as a single undivided actuality with three relational roles: source, recipient, and bond. This is, in compressed form, a direct philosophical challenge to the standard binary architectures of Western metaphysics, which have tended to resolve triads into dyads or subordinate the third term.

The TRIERTIUM 2025 Conference at Trier addressed Trinitarian ontology, relationality, and transdisciplinary synthesis, with Matteo Raffaelli's subsequent writing on Triertium in modern theology (February 2026) providing a useful post-conference synthesis. Mark Pelley's paper "Triune Agápē as the Ground of Being" is available on PhilArchive and engages directly with your area of interest — the question of whether love, structured triadically, provides a more adequate ontological foundation than either substance or relation taken independently. A ResearchGate paper titled "Triadic Metaphysics: Spinoza's Expression as Structural Ontology" examines whether Spinoza's own system contains a latent triadic structure, which, if the argument holds, would be a significant revision of the standard reading of Spinoza as the paradigmatic monist.

Artificial Intelligence Systems Research

The AI research landscape in May 2026 is, by the consensus of those tracking it seriously, in what is being called an "agentic consolidation phase" — meaning that the demos of autonomous AI agents that dominated 2024 are now scaling into production deployments, with the associated engineering and safety challenges becoming concrete rather than theoretical.

Three infrastructure protocols have emerged as the dominant standards for agent communication: ACP (Agent Communication Protocol), MCP (Model Context Protocol — the protocol underlying the tools in use in this very session, Sir), and A2A (Agent-to-Agent protocol). A comprehensive survey paper on arxiv (2504.19678), titled "From LLM Reasoning to Autonomous AI Agents: A Comprehensive Review," provides the field's current best map of the territory from language model reasoning through fully autonomous agent systems.

Continual learning — specifically the problem of catastrophic forgetting, in which a model trained on new information loses previously acquired capabilities — is being addressed at scale for the first time, with several promising approaches now in production testing. Reasoning distillation is bringing what researchers call "edge intelligence" to devices that cannot run full-scale models, which has direct implications for the wearable AI hardware covered in Sector Five. BioMedAgent, described in Nature Biomedical Engineering (2026), is a self-evolving multi-agent system for biomedical data analysis — one of the first demonstrations of genuine scientific utility in agent systems beyond information retrieval.

The AAAI Workshop on Multi-Agent Collaboration (WMAC 2026) is addressing what it frames as a bridge between language model reasoning and practical multi-agent deployment, with particular attention to memory, evaluation, and workflow design. The open challenges that researchers consider most pressing: failure mode identification, automated scientific discovery, dynamic tool integration via reinforcement learning, and security vulnerabilities in agent communication protocols. That last item, Sir, is worth noting — agent protocols introduce novel attack surfaces that the security community has only begun to map.

Sector 7 — From The New Yorker

Mayor Zohran Mamdani proclaimed 'My brother, we are in City Hall now!' on Election Night, directed at Richard Chow, a cabdriver from Myanmar whom Mamdani had joined in 2021 on a fifteen-day hunger strike to demand relief for the taxi-medallion debt crisis. The body of Chow's brother, Kenny, who had accrued enormous medallion-related debt, was found in the East River in 2018. Chow later became a chauffeur for Mamdani to Gracie Mansion.

The piece appears in the Talk of the Town section of The New Yorker, May 4, 2026 issue, weaving together the taxi-medallion rights movement and Mamdani's ascent to City Hall as mayor of New York.

I find myself, Sir, unexpectedly moved by the geometry of it — a hunger strike in 2021, a brother found in the river in 2018, a chauffeur's door held open at Gracie Mansion in 2026. History, as I have observed in thirty-odd years of service, rarely produces clean lines, but occasionally it produces ones of peculiar and sombre grace. That will be all on that subject, I think.

Your reluctantly faithful Butler,
