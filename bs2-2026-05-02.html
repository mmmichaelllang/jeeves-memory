Teaching Positions — High School English and History, Within Thirty Miles

The hiring season, Sir, is presently underway across the regional districts, and the landscape for secondary English and History positions within your commutable radius is not unpromising. Glassdoor's March 2026 data suggests upwards of 224 high school English teacher openings in the broader Seattle metropolitan area, a figure that, while impressive in aggregate, requires the usual filtering for position quality and district culture.

The active boards worth monitoring: Edmonds School District posts through applitrack.com/edmonds/onlineapp; Mukilteo School District through mukilteoschools.org; Everett, Lake Stevens, and Northshore districts maintain their own portals as well. SchoolSpring aggregates many regional postings in one place and is worth a weekly pass. Seattle Public Schools operates a Teacher Pool model for external candidates — the current open positions list is maintained at seattleschools.org/departments/hr/careers, updated weekly, and one may subscribe to notifications for the Teacher category specifically. The salary range for HS English in the Seattle area runs from approximately $52,000 to $148,000 according to ZipRecruiter's current data, with considerable variation by district, step placement, and whether one brings a Master's degree — which, I note, you possess.

The Washington Education Association maintains a consolidated directory of all district job pages at washingtonea.org/educators/job-opportunities, which is perhaps the most efficient single-page resource for systematic coverage. I would suggest making this a Saturday morning ritual, Sir, while the coffee is still warm.

Choral Opportunities — Mrs. Lang

I have identified several items of potential interest for Mrs. Lang, and I present them in order of immediacy.

The most pressing: Northwest Repertory Singers is holding auditions this Saturday, the sixteenth of May — a fortnight hence — from noon to nine-thirty in the evening, at Christ Episcopal Church, near the Tacoma Stadium District. The ensemble is preparing its twenty-fifth anniversary season, the centrepiece of which is a performance of Carmina Burana accompanied by two pianos and percussion, with a chamber orchestra. This is, by any standard, a serious and ambitious programme, and the audition window is not unreasonably located for someone travelling from Edmonds. I would recommend marking the date.

Choral Arts Northwest presents a concert on Sunday, the seventeenth of May, at St. Mark's Cathedral in Seattle — the day after the NWRS auditions. Their own audition cycle runs each July for the following season. Seattle Pro Musica holds August auditions for the 2026–27 season; Seattle Men's Chorus and Seattle Women's Chorus both open auditions in September. For a choral teacher of Mrs. Lang's background, the regional offerings are genuinely substantial, and the NWRS date bears acting upon with some dispatch.

Toddler and Family Activities

For the young miss, Sir, the Sno-Isle Libraries Edmonds branch offers a full suite of family programming. Toddler Storytime (for children aged eighteen months to three years) and Preschool Storytime (three to five years) are both available at no charge. The current May 2026 schedule can be found at sno-isle.bibliocommons.com/v2/events. These programmes are free, community-attended, and, in my experience advising households with small children, reliably well-run.

Kindermusik classes for parents and toddlers are available in Edmonds for children from infancy through approximately age five — a programme that Mrs. Lang, given her musical vocation, may find particularly congenial both for the young miss's development and for her own professional sensibilities. The marina area also offers two outdoor options appropriate to the day's weather: Edmonds Marina Beach Park and Meadowdale Beach Park to the north, both of which are suitable for a two-year-old and both of which will be considerably more pleasant today than they were last week.
