I note, Sir, that the wider situation in Washington and Tehran appears — against all probabilistic expectation — to be trending toward negotiation rather than kinetic escalation this morning. What passes for diplomatic progress in the second quarter of this century is a subject I shall address in Sector Three. For now, I shall simply observe that the region is engaged, as ever, in a state of total, unmitigated shittery — I do beg your pardon, Sir. The professional language will improve presently.

The Weather

The morning opened at fifty-one degrees Fahrenheit with an overcast sky — the particular grey of the Pacific Northwest that manages to be both contemplative and slightly oppressive simultaneously. The forecast offers a genuine promise of improvement: clouds are expected to give way to sun as the morning progresses, and the high today reaches sixty-two degrees, with light south-southeasterly winds. This is, by Edmonds standards, a satisfactory Thursday in May.

Tonight's low holds at fifty degrees, and tomorrow follows with mostly sunny skies and highs again in the low sixties. In short, Mister Lang: it is a fine day for a walk along the waterfront if the afternoon schedule allows it, and I would not discourage you.

Edmonds & Municipal Affairs

The local calendar holds several items worthy of your attention — and potentially Mrs. Lang's, given her musical sensibilities.

The Edmonds Jazz Connection convenes this Friday, May 9th, for what is described as a full-day event celebrating young jazz musicians in the area. Given Mrs. Lang's background in music education, this seems an event whose atmosphere might suit the household broadly, and it is certainly the sort of thing a two-year-old attends with minimal scheduling risk.

The SketcherFest Edmonds 2026 poster has been unveiled, this year featuring the work of David Chamness — a Shoreline-based artist and retired architect — whose composition centres on the Fifth Avenue and Main Street roundabout. The roundabout, I feel, is an appropriate subject for an art event; it captures the slightly circular nature of municipal life.

The Edmonds Driftwood Players open their production of Agatha Christie's The Mysterious Affair at Styles on May 15th, running through June 7th at the Wade James Theatre. Christie's first novel — and the debut of Hercule Poirot, as you no doubt recall — is a reliably satisfying choice for a community company, demanding precision of dialogue while rewarding attentive audiences. Should you and Mrs. Lang wish an evening out in the coming weeks, this strikes me as agreeably low-risk entertainment.

The Edmonds Center for the Arts presents a pair of concerts in quick succession: the Wailin' Jennys perform Saturday, May 16th, and Alan Doyle follows on Sunday, May 17th. Both are acts with devoted followings, and if your calendar has a free Saturday evening, the Jennys are worth considering.

The Cascadia Art Museum offers free admission on the third Thursday of each month; the next such Thursday is May 21st, with a five o'clock tour. I mention this now so that it may be calendared in advance rather than recalled too late, as is the usual fate of free cultural events.

The City of Edmonds is currently accepting applications for its 2027 Lodging Tax Fund, for those operating in the hospitality or tourism sectors. The Edmonds Food Bank's GiveBIG and Stamp Out Hunger campaigns remain active. And Edmonds College has scheduled its Triton Visit Day for May 15th, from two to five in the afternoon; interested parties may register at outreach@edmonds.edu.

Public Safety

No new crime report for May 7th within the relevant three-mile radius. Edmonds continues to carry an A-minus crime grade. The year-over-year picture bears noting: violent crime is down 0.9 percent, which is welcome; property crime is up 14.6 percent, which is less so. The city remains, by any comparative standard, a remarkably safe community — but the property crime trend warrants attention, particularly for a household with a vehicle in a driveway and a garage serving as an improvised workshop. Nothing actionable today, Sir, but worth holding in mind.
