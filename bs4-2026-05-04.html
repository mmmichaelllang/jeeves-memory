Triadic Ontology and Theological Physics

Research this week surfaces several points of active scholarly engagement with the ontological frameworks that have occupied your philosophical attention, Sir.

The Triertium Research Group at Olomouc held an April 2025 seminar on "Erwin Schadel and the Bamberg School of Triadic Ontology" (presented by Raffaelli), and a June 2025 session specifically on Structural Ontic Realism and Trinitarian ontologies. The related volume, Trinitarian Ontologies, volume one, edited by Eduard Fiedler, is in circulation — a reference worth securing if you have not already done so.

The PhilPapers index for 2026 flags an entry for what is designated "Eventist-Infinitesimal Ontology" — characterising entities as fundamentally events rather than substances, constituted by what the authors term infinitely divisible relationality. The authors draw an explicit speculative analogy with divine perichoresis for purposes of theistic realism, and the abstract includes the formulation "Trinitarian metaphysics affirming irreducible relationality of all being." The connection to your working notes on participation and ontological constitution will, I trust, be immediately visible without requiring elaboration on my part.

The Perichoresis journal (2022) offers a formulation worth having to hand: "the tripartite nature of perichoresis presents an irreducible relational dynamic which enables the polarities of a relationship to remain distinct entities while allowing each to be constituted from within the formative matrix of the relational unity itself." Perichoresis.org's current eleven-week online course, "Introducing the Trinitarian Faith 2026," runs through June 2026 under the direction of C. Baxter Kruger, for whatever that is worth as a primary or supplementary resource.

The Aeon piece noted in Sector 3 — Pina-Cabral on Lévy-Bruhl's participatory ontology — warrants a second mention here in the context of this specific enquiry. The proposition that personhood is constituted through participation rather than prior to it maps, with interesting imprecision, onto perichoretic ontology's claim that relational constitution is more fundamental than individual substance. Whether this convergence is more than structural analogy — whether it implies a deeper common intuition about the nature of being that cuts across phenomenology, theology, and philosophical anthropology — seems worth pursuing in the notes, Sir, if you have not already pursued it there.

AI Systems Research — 2026 Landscape

The multi-agent pipeline architecture that characterises current AI systems research combines large language model reasoning with dynamic prompting, hierarchical memory under the designation HiMem (using semantic segmentation and reconsolidation), and structured tool use. The principal frameworks in active deployment are CrewAI, AutoGen, OpenAgents, and SuperAGI — each addressing different aspects of the coordination and orchestration problem that arises when multiple AI agents must collaborate on tasks that exceed the scope of a single model context.

Anthropic's Claude Managed Agents, covered in Wired (2026), provides a managed harness with memory, tool access, and sandboxed execution — a significant step toward safe, reliable agentic deployment at production scale. The practical implications for AI-assisted professional work across research, legal, medical, and educational domains are substantial.

Prompt caching currently enables reductions of up to ninety percent in token consumption for retrieval-augmented generation workflows — a development with significant cost and efficiency implications for any organisation running LLM pipelines at scale. The AdaPlan-H approach, combining coarse macro-planning with adaptive iterative refinement, represents one of the more sophisticated current answers to the planning horizon problem in agent systems. Meta-prompting and prompt folding allow large language models to generate task-specific prompts dynamically, reducing the overhead of manual prompt engineering for recurring task types.

The evaluation landscape is maturing accordingly: REALM-Bench, ChatEval, and LangGraph now provide structured frameworks for assessing multi-agent performance across a range of task types and coordination patterns. The risk register for these systems warrants equal attention to the performance benchmarks: hallucination propagation across agent chains, alignment degradation under extended tool use, emergent collusion between agents operating toward misaligned sub-goals, and supply-chain security vulnerabilities in third-party tool integrations are all active concerns in the current literature and none of them is purely theoretical at this stage of deployment.

Pedagogical Innovation — AI-Assisted Teaching

For the classroom dimension of this enquiry, Sir: SchoolAI reports average time savings of 5.9 hours per week for teachers using the platform — translating to approximately six weeks of recovered instructional planning time over an academic year, which is a figure that bears repeating and bears comparing to the number of planning periods most districts actually provide. More than eighty percent of teachers using AI assistance for worksheet generation report significant savings; seventy-four percent note equivalent efficiency when AI is used to differentiate materials across student need levels. AI implementation in schools has increased more than forty percent in the past year, suggesting the early adopter phase is giving way to something more broadly institutional.

The four-step AI lesson planning methodology that has emerged from practitioner experience involves grounding in content standards; unpacking those standards with AI assistance; iterative refinement using the ACDQ framework (Act, Context, Depth, Questions); and human editorial review. This structure preserves teacher professional judgment at the standards and review stages while delegating the generation of draft materials to the AI — which is, if one may venture an opinion, the correct division of labour. The AI generates; the teacher evaluates, contextualises, and decides.

The Hello History ChatGPT application, which allows students to conduct direct-message conversations with historical figures, merits particular note for a History teacher: the pedagogical potential for exploring perspective, voice, and the constructed nature of historical narrative is considerable. The scaffold that makes the difference between a meaningful exercise and an uncritical simulation belongs to the teacher — which is, of course, where it always did. The 74 (May 2026) published a list of ten useful technology tools for educators that may be worth reviewing for any overlooked resources in this space.

UAP Disclosure

A Presidential Directive issued in February 2026 ordered the identification and release of all government UAP, UFO, and related files, with the Pentagon confirming cooperation through the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office. Fortune (May third, 2026) confirmed the directive's scope and the Pentagon's compliance posture.

The 2025 Congressional hearings on unidentified aerial phenomena included testimony from military veterans presenting MQ-9 drone footage of a Hellfire missile engaged against a high-speed orb — a documented incident that prompted renewed demands for sensor standardisation across military platforms and expanded whistleblower protection for personnel with relevant knowledge. The UAP Records Collection at the National Archives and Records Administration was mandated by the 2024 National Defense Authorization Act and is proceeding accordingly.

AARO's official position remains that no evidence of extraterrestrial origin has been established, while acknowledging anomalous performance characteristics in certain documented encounters. Former intelligence officer David Grusch's claim that the government possesses what he characterises as "non-human biologics" from alleged crash retrieval sites remains an unresolved thread in the public record — neither confirmed nor refuted by the institutional disclosure process currently underway. The Guardian (April twenty-second, 2026) reported on newly declassified Pentagon UAP footage as part of the ongoing release.
