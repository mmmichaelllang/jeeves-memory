Sector 4 — Specific Enquiries

Theological Physics and Triadic Ontology

The field of triadic ontology — the philosophical and theological inquiry into structures of threefold being, often in dialogue with Trinitarian theology — has not, I must confess, produced a surge of new scholarship in the May 2026 search window. The specialist literature moves slowly and deliberately, as befits a discipline that is attempting to reconcile the fundamental structure of reality with the inner life of God. One does not rush such things.

The primary ongoing series remains the Verlag Karl Alber volumes on Studies on Triadic Ontology and Trinitarian Philosophy, edited by Enders, Falque, Milbank, Coda, and Rojek — a transconfessional scholarly collaboration that continues to define the field's methodological horizon. The TRIERTIUM initiative, connecting the universities of Trier and Olomouc, is running its ongoing seminar programme on Erwin Schadel's Bamberg School and Structural Ontic Realism — Schadel's attempt to ground a rigorous triadic metaphysics in formal-logical as well as theological terms.

Damiano Migliorini's work continues to attract attention in analytic theology circles: his treatment of the divine persons as "subsisting relations" in the Thomistic sense, brought into dialogue with contemporary analytic ontology, offers a bridge between medieval precision and modern analytical rigour. On the speculative end, the Eventist-Infinitesimal Ontology framework (documented on PhilPapers) proposes entities as fundamentally events with irreducibly triadic relationality — and draws an explicit speculative analogy with the theological concept of perichoresis, the mutual indwelling of the divine persons. Whether one finds this analogy illuminating or merely suggestive will depend on one's tolerance for formal-speculative theology, but it is at least a serious attempt.

Of immediate diary note: a Rome conference titled "Nature Lost, Nature Regained" is scheduled for June 2026, convened by the New Trinitarian Ontologies network, TRIERTIUM, and St. Mary Seminary. This represents the field's primary conference event of the spring season and should be on your radar, Sir, even if attendance is not practicable.

Artificial Intelligence — Systems and Agents

The AI multi-agent research landscape continues its rapid evolution. The GitHub repository masamasa59/ai-agent-papers (1,300 stars and growing) is tracking the field on a biweekly basis; its current summary identifies deep research agents, skill acquisition, memory architectures, and self-evolving agents as the dominant research themes of the moment.

The ACM Conference on AI Systems (CAIS) 2026 has produced a significant paper on compound LLM agent design in adversarial partially observable Markov decision process (POMDP) environments — a mouthful that translates to: how do you build multi-step AI agents that remain robust when they cannot see the full state of the world and when adversarial conditions obtain? The paper proposes a three-layer architecture of context management, reasoning, and hierarchical decision-making. Glia, a research initiative focused on human-inspired AI, is applying similar principles to automated systems design and optimisation.

BCG has published its "OpenClaw and the New Wave of AI Agents" report; the CogitX framework articulates a full-loop agent design cycle: perception → reasoning → planning → memory → action → orchestration. The Model Context Protocol (MCP) remains the dominant interoperability standard for multi-agent workflows, with LangGraph, CrewAI, and LlamaIndex as the leading frameworks for building against it. Governance considerations — human-in-the-loop design, kill switches, permission systems, and risk tiering — are receiving increasing attention as deployed agent systems grow in complexity and autonomy.

I note, Sir, that this field moves at a pace that makes even a week's lag in reading feel like a meaningful disadvantage. The biweekly tracking repository is worth bookmarking.

Pedagogical Innovation

The intersection of artificial intelligence and classroom practice has produced several findings worth your attention as a teacher candidate.

A 2024–25 K-12 survey reports that 60% of teachers used AI in their practice — up from 46% the prior year. SchoolAI users report saving an average of 5.9 hours per week; MagicSchool (which offers more than 80 distinct tools) reports 7–10 hours per week saved among active users. 74% of surveyed teachers report using AI to differentiate instruction more effectively. 86% report using AI detection tools, though experts warn of significant inaccuracy rates — a point that teachers employing these tools for academic honesty enforcement ought to weight carefully.

On the lesson planning side: GradeWithAI offers a free, standards-aligned history lesson plan generator using an I Do/We Do/You Do instructional model. SchoolAI provides high school templates emphasising critical thinking, inquiry-driven learning, complex texts, and Socratic seminar formats. Hello History allows students to conduct text-based conversations with historical figures — a tool with obvious applications in your subject areas. The ACM CAIS 2026 compound agent paper has direct educational systems implications, as does the Glia human-inspired AI project's work on educational systems design.

The MCP protocol's traction as an interoperability standard has specific relevance for building AI-enhanced classroom workflows — enabling a teacher to, for instance, connect a lesson planning tool, a grading assistant, and a student interaction logger through a common interface.

UAP Disclosure

The unidentified aerial phenomena landscape in April–May 2026 is, to borrow a phrase from the intelligence community, target-rich but evidence-poor.

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) published its FY2025 report on April 9th, 2026. The report documents more than 2,000 active cases and states, flatly, that there is no conclusive extraterrestrial evidence in the current case inventory. Former AARO director Kirkpatrick has reiterated publicly: "no bombshell alien evidence." The office's credibility with disclosure advocates is not high, and a Senate Intelligence Committee hearing on April 22nd pressed AARO representatives on classification practices.

The White House registered alien.gov and aliens.gov as symbolic UAP transparency gestures following a February 2026 presidential directive; DefenseScoop reported on the domain registrations in March. As of May 2026, both sites remain dark. The gap between the symbolic gesture and any substantive content is, one observes, considerable.

A House Oversight Committee hearing is scheduled for May 12th — a week from today. Lawmakers are demanding the release of 46 specific UAP videos; a deadline of April 14th was missed without explanation or consequence. Whistleblowers have claimed that AARO holds undisclosed video recordings. Businessman Robert Bigelow has made unverified claims regarding the acquisition of non-human material. Vice President JD Vance, according to reporting, is described as "obsessed" with the UFO question and holds the view that the phenomena may be demonic in nature — which is at minimum a novel theological-ufological synthesis. President Trump on April 29th hinted at an "imminent release" of UAP files, which is the kind of statement that has been made with some regularity since 2017 without producing the promised revelations. The Guardian's April 12th coverage quotes UAP watchers as saying they have "never been closer" to significant evidence. One notes the phrase "never been closer" has itself been in circulation for approximately fifty years.
