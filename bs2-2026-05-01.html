The Professional Horizon — Teaching Positions

I have surveyed the employment landscape for High School English and History positions within a thirty-mile radius, Sir, and the picture is, characteristically for this season, a mixture of genuine opportunity and the usual statistical noise of aggregator platforms padding their numbers with duplicates and stale listings.

The most substantive concrete posting identified during the research phase comes from Cascade Christian Schools in Puyallup, which listed a Teacher — High School English position for the 2026–27 school year on 16 April 2026, as documented on edjoblist.com. Puyallup sits at the outer edge of the thirty-mile radius from Edmonds, but the posting is current and specific, which already places it above the generality of what the aggregators are serving up.

Glassdoor reports 540 High School English teacher positions statewide in Washington, while Indeed lists 117 in the Snohomish County area specifically and 136 in the broader Seattle region. ZipRecruiter, which traffics in somewhat more optimistic figures, places the count at over 1,000 positions in the Seattle area, with salary ranges from $52,000 to $124,000 annually. The average yearly compensation for a High School English teacher in Seattle, per ZipRecruiter as of 28 April 2026, is $67,757. Glassdoor's Seattle-area figure of 317 positions, and Indeed's 120, bracket this rather more conservatively.

For Seattle Public Schools specifically, the research phase was unable to pull direct district postings for the current cycle — I would direct you to seattleschools.org/careers for a direct review of openings for the 2026–27 school year. There was also a Founding Math/Science Lead Teacher posting in Tukwila at $63,000–$91,000, which I mention only for completeness and suspect is not a match for your particular expertise.

For Mrs. Lang — Choral Auditions

The choral calendar presents several items of genuine interest, and I commend them to Mrs. Lang's attention with my usual respect for her musical judgment.

The most time-sensitive matter is Opus 7 Vocal Ensemble, a forty-member adult auditioned community choir operating under the umbrella of the Greater Seattle Choral Consortium (listed at seattlesings.org). Audition dates are scheduled for Monday the 4th of May and Wednesday the 6th of May, with the ensemble's next performance event on the 16th of May. Given that Monday is three days hence, I would suggest that if this option appeals, the matter of scheduling an audition slot warrants attention today rather than after the weekend.

The Seattle Choral Company holds open auditions on an ongoing basis, meeting at Phinney Ridge Lutheran Church. Dues are structured at $225 for the full season or $30 per month. The Seattle Men's Chorus administers its audition weekends through Theatre Puget Sound. The Seattle Opera maintains a chorus for Washington State residents between the ages of 18 and 30, should that demographic apply.
